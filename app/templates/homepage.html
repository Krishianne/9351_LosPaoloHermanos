Coffee is one of the most widely consumed beverages in the world and plays a vital role in many national economies, including that of the Philippines (PCAARRD, n.d.). Its global demand has given rise to a highly competitive industry where quality assessment is essential in maintaining standards across the supply chain. One important aspect of this process is coffee bean grading, a systematic approach to evaluating coffee quality that enables producers, traders, and consumers to align expectations and ensure fair market practices (Zwart, 2021). Coffee grade directly influences a product's market value, consumer appeal, and standing in international trade.
