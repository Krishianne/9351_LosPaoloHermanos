Grade-A, Grade-AA, Grade-AAA, Grade-AB, Grade-C:

These grades represent the different levels of quality which is determined by a coffee beanâ€™s size and number of defects. The higher grade (Grade-AAA) indicates that the coffee bean has a larger size with minimal defects. In contrast, lower grade (Grade-C) indicates that the coffee bean has a smaller size and more defects (Sucaf ina: Plantation AA, n.d.-b).

PB-I and PB-II

The PB for these gradings stands for Peaberry. This type of grading refers to the coffee beans that occur when a single bean develops inside the coffee (Nair, 2022). PB-I coffee beans are considered as a higher-quality grade, while PB-II refers to a lower-quality grade (Sucaf ina: Plantation AA, n.d.-b).
